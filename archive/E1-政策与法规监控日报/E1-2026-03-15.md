## Answer

The latest trends in supply chain restructuring include nearshoring, friend-shoring, and reshoring. The United States has shown a significant trend towards reshoring, particularly in high-end manufacturing industries, driven by government incentives and a desire to bring production closer to markets. Nearshoring has also gained traction, with companies opting to relocate operations to neighboring countries to enhance supply chain efficiency and stability. Friend-shoring, or ally-shoring, involves relocating production to politically allied nations to mitigate risks associated with geopolitical tensions and supply chain disruptions. Recent data indicates that the United States has accelerated its reshoring and nearshoring efforts, while Europe has focused on nearshoring with mixed results in friend-shoring. Taiwan is emerging as a potential ally-shoring partner for U.S. companies seeking alternatives to China and India due to its high regulatory trust level.
