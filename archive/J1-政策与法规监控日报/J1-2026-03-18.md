In contrast, China's PIPL, enacted in 2021, applies to any organization processing personal information of Chinese citizens, regardless of the organization's location. The European Data Protection Board (EDPB) has highlighted the differences in government access to data between the GDPR and PIPL, noting that the PIPL allows for more extensive government access to personal data compared to the GDPR.

The global supply chain is undergoing significant restructuring characterized by three main trends: reshoring, nearshoring, and friend-shoring. Reshoring, or the return of manufacturing to home countries, has been notably driven by the United States, particularly in high-tech manufacturing sectors. Nearshoring, which involves relocating production to geographically closer countries, has seen significant growth in the United States and Europe, with Mexico and countries in Central and Eastern Europe becoming major beneficiaries. Friend-shoring, or relocating production to politically allied nations, has accelerated post-2022, with the United States focusing on Southeast Asian and European countries to reduce dependency on geopolitical rivals. These trends reflect efforts by developed nations to regain control over their supply chains and mitigate geopolitical risks.
