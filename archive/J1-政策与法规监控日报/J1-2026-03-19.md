## Answer

The global supply chain is undergoing significant restructuring characterized by three main trends: reshoring, nearshoring, and friend-shoring. Reshoring, or the return of manufacturing to home countries, is notably prominent in the United States, particularly in high-tech manufacturing sectors. Nearshoring, or outsourcing to geographically closer countries, has seen significant growth in the United States and Europe, with Mexico, Central and Eastern Europe, and North Africa as key beneficiaries. Friend-shoring, or relocating production to politically allied nations, has accelerated post-2022, especially in the United States, with Southeast Asia and Europe as major regions of focus. These trends reflect efforts to enhance supply chain resilience and reduce geopolitical risks.
